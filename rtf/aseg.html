Ivy Road to Fontaine

This is one of the longest stretches of uninterrupted trail along the entire Rivanna Trail Loop. Rolling hills, rocks, roots, footbridges, and occasional mud pits can be found along this section. Wanderers can branch off to the large network of trails on UVA's "O-Hill".

Directions

  • Ivy to Fontaine
  • Fontaine to Ivy
Hop on the trail right next to the fire station, and then just follow the trail. Stay right anywhere you're not sure where to go, and you won't get lost. Or, for the more adventurous, branch off to the left anywhere in the "O-Hill" area, in the second half of this leg.
Across the street from the Fontaine Research Park, just before Fontaine Ave hits the 29 Bypass, the trail heads up a rocky slope (not the gravel driveway, left of that). Stay left at any intersection you're not sure of (or wander right for more trails!), and you'll trace a route right along the bypass, and won't get lost. You'll finish on a little uphill, after a bridge over a creek, at the Ivy Road fire station.

North From Here

From the trailhead at Ivy Rd (point A on the map above), your goal is basically to get to the continuation of the trail on the opposite side of Ivy Rd and across Old Ivy Rd from UVA's Printing and Copying Center. Please note, however, that it is illegal to cross the train tracks. You can walk along Ivy to the East (towards Charlottesville) until it meets Old Ivy at a traffic light, and then turn left and double back. Alternatively, you can walk along Ivy to the West (away from Charlottesville), turn right to cross under the train tracks, and turn right again to follow Old Ivy.
