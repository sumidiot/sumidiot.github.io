Fontaine to Stribling

A half mile with evergreens, floodplains, bamboo, and a creek crossing!

Click for full, interactive map

Directions

  • Fontaine to Stribling

Walk under pines, cross a fence stile, follow road across bridge, turn left to enter the floodplains of Morey Creek. Cross the creek on stepping stones. The adjoining Department of Forestry Nature Trail spur (1 mile) contains several trees unusual to Virginia, remnants of a 1930s tree nursery. Use caution crossing the creek.

The trail picks back up directly opposite Fontaine Ave, in a patch of woods. It winds up a hill, and then back down, in an area with lots of evergreens, and a nice soft surface. Owing to the lack of undergrowth, the trail may seem ill-defined at times, as it meanders through the trees, but it's usually pretty easy to follow. On its way back down the hill, you'll end up crossing a fence on a wooden ladder. The trail then climbs back up to the road, and you want to continue on the road down the hill. Just after crossing a creek on the road, look for the trail on your left. Head down the embankment, and follow the trail, which keeps the creek on your left. There's a spur to your right that will take you up the hill, and comes back in and connects with the main trail just before the trail crosses the creek. At the creek crossing, the creek will head through a tunnel under some railroad tracks, and you can either rock-hop across, or walk across the black pipe. On the other side of the creek, the trail continues through a bamboo grove, and you should watch your feet for bamboo that was cut a few inches above ground level. The trail comes out to Stribling Rd, which is a dirt road, in short order.

The Forestry Loop

This 1-mile spur trail is part of the Department of Forestry, and contains several trees unusual to Virginia, remnants of a 1930s tree nursery.

When coming from Fontaine, at the road, just after crossing the bridge over the creek, stay on the road and look for a trailhead on your right to enter the loop (or, if you drop down on to the trail just after the bridge, look for a spur trail to your right about half-way to the creek crossing). Follow the trail counter-clockwise, finishing with a few switchbacks to join the main trail at the creek crossing.
